## 4. Precision Machine Shop (~10 years, dates TBD)
- Role: Mechanical Designer / Drafter — with extensive shop floor integration
- **Design work:** Top-tier gearboxes (planetary) and firearm components
- **Assembly:** Hands-on — pressing pins into planetary gearbox planets, bushings into cages and annuli. This direct assembly experience informed design tolerancing decisions.
- **Machine operation:** Twin turret dual spindle 7-axis mill-turn centers — operator part inspections, tool offsetting for wear, tool changes
- **Programming:** 3D toolpath programming in milling department when lead programmer was overloaded. Partial setups. Not 4th-axis or higher, but more hands-on than almost any other drafter/designer/engineer in the building.
- **Inspection:** High-end digital and programmatic height gages, sin blocks, dial indicators, datum tables — inspecting features most shops would need a CMM for. Built clever setups with a Micro Height + sin blocks + dial indicator along part geometry when more sophisticated tooling wasn't available. Could inspect it because he understood *why* features were called out that way.
- **GD&T:** Applied properly because he had to assemble parts into sub-assemblies requiring accuracy like circular runout of a precision bore < .0015" — vibration at high RPM would cause catastrophic failures.
- **Heat treat knowledge:** Different effects depending on material, hardness, tolerance requirements. Managing thin-wall distortion adjacent to large cross-sections. Leaving just enough stock for post-heat-treat finish ops to hit ±.0005" tolerance while minimizing cost.
- **Quoting:** Single parts to full 100-piece assemblies (industrial pneumatic torque wrenches). Understanding the balance between durability and cost — not designing everything out of "unobtanium" (C300, 300M) while keeping material cost reasonable.
- **Material procurement:** Created spreadsheets and small databases to calculate all material across all parts/assemblies/open orders. Issued blanket POs across multiple vendors and eventually direct from mills.
