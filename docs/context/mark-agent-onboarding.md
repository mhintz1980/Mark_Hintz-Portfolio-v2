# Mark Agent Onboarding

Purpose: fast orientation for future agents working on Mark Hintz's portfolio, messaging, case studies, or Remotion content.

## Who Mark Is

Mark should not be framed as a generic CAD designer, generic engineer, or generic "mechanical + software" hybrid.

The strongest framing is:

`Design + Manufacturing Bridge`

Supporting differentiators:

- tolerance and inspection authority
- lifecycle systems thinking
- practical cost and manufacturability judgment

Mark's value comes from direct exposure across the product lifecycle, especially where design meets manufacturing reality.

He has meaningful context in:

- CAD and mechanical design
- shop-floor realities
- machine setup and programming support
- assembly constraints
- inspection and metrology
- GD&T and tolerance reasoning
- quoting and material-cost tradeoffs
- lifecycle thinking

This means his work should feel authored by someone who understands why parts fail, how prints are used, how assemblies fight you, and why design decisions must survive fabrication, assembly, and use.

## Positioning Rules

Primary message:

`Mechanical design with manufacturing reality built in.`

Secondary support language:

- `CAD informed by assembly, inspection, tooling, and cost`
- `Designs that survive machining, heat treat, tolerance stack, and use`
- `From concept to floor-ready documentation`

Important constraint:

- software and automation should stay visible, but secondary
- they are operational leverage and systems support
- they are not the dominant identity

Avoid framing Mark as:

- software-first
- innovation-theater systems thinker
- generic "full-stack maker"
- abstract consultant detached from the floor

## Visual Direction Rules

The preferred visual direction is:

`Industrial / Technical / Blueprint`

But not as superficial decoration.

The visual system should feel like it came from someone who understands:

- drawing packages
- revision control
- datum references
- callouts
- setup constraints
- assembly order
- inspection logic
- manufacturing tradeoffs

The reel and portfolio should feel closer to:

- engineering review deck
- technical presentation
- blueprint-led design narrative

And less like:

- startup promo
- flashy product reel
- generic motion-design showcase

## How Mark Likes To Work

Mark wants to iterate quickly and visually.

Useful working assumptions:

- he likes to get close fast, then dial in details
- Studio editability is valuable because it reduces context switching
- Remotion's programmatic nature is a feature, not just a rendering tool
- he may create many variants, keep some for the portfolio, and reuse others later
- he is open to experimentation, but the work still needs to respect the core positioning

Practical implication for agents:

- favor editable controls when they remove repeated file-hopping
- keep systems flexible enough to support multiple video variants
- avoid burying important content decisions across many disconnected files
